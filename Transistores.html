Transistors

What is a Transistor?
A transistor is a type of semiconductor device that can be used to conduct and insulate electric current or voltage. A transistor basically acts as a switch and an amplifier. In simple words, we can say that a transistor is a miniature device that is used to control or regulate the flow of electronic signals.

  • BJT (Bipolar Junction Transistor): Controlled by current. Common types are NPN and PNP. Used for switching and amplification.
  • MOSFET (Metal-Oxide Semiconductor FET): Controlled by voltage. Very efficient and widely used in modern electronics.
  • JFET (Junction FET): Voltage-controlled like MOSFETs, but older and simpler.
  • IGBT: Used in high-power applications such as motor controllers and inverters.
